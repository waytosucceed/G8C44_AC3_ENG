Key Takeaways

Personification
Personification is a writing technique that attributes human qualities or emotions to things, objects, or ideas that are not human. This device helps paint vivid pictures and elicits feelings from the reader.

Type 1: Emotional Personification
Emotional Personification involves assigning human emotions to non-human entities to create a deeper emotional impact. For example, “Anxiety tapped me on the shoulder, refusing to let me forget the ticking clock,” conveys how persistent anxiety can feel like a constant presence.

Type 2: Anthropomorphic Personification
Anthropomorphic Personification gives human-like traits and actions to animals or objects. For instance, “The old book stretched its spine, eager to share its hidden tales with the curious reader,” personifies the book as if it longs to communicate its stories.

Type 3: Allegorical Personification
Allegorical Personification turns abstract ideas into characters that deliver moral lessons. For example, “Curiosity burst through the door, pulling the children away from their screens and into the world, teaching them that the greatest adventures often lie just outside their comfort zone,” portrays Curiosity as an inviting character that encourages exploration and discovery.

Type 4: Extended Personification
Extended Personification develops a single object’s human qualities over a longer narrative. For example, “The moon wore a silver crown as it danced among the stars, sharing gentle light with the sleeping world below, cradling dreams in its soft glow,” illustrates how the moon is depicted with human-like elegance and nurturing qualities, emphasizing its calming presence in the night sky.

Words that I Learnt from the Story

Sage:
Meaning: A wise person, often associated with profound knowledge or good judgment; can also refer to a plant used for seasoning or in herbal medicine.
Example: "The village sought the advice of the sage, hoping his wisdom would guide them through the difficult times."

Rustling:
Meaning: The soft, muffled sound made by movement, often associated with leaves, papers, or fabric.
Example: "The rustling of the leaves in the gentle breeze created a soothing background as we sat in the garden."

Loomed:
Meaning: To appear in a large or imposing form, often creating a sense of danger or threat; can also refer to something that is imminent or about to happen.
Example: "As the storm clouds rolled in, dark shadows loomed over the horizon, signaling the impending rain."

Deliberately:
Meaning: Done with intention, purpose, or careful consideration; not done accidentally or hastily.
Example: "She deliberately placed the vase on the shelf, ensuring it was centered and perfectly aligned with the other decorations."
